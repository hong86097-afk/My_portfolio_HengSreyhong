Who · Behind the work

About

I’m drawn to the part of a problem where raw, messy data first starts to make sense. Most of my work lives at the intersection of statistics and machine learning — building models that predict, classify, or forecast, then pressure-testing them until the results are honest.

I like clean pipelines, readable code, and visuals that make a finding obvious to someone seeing it for the first time. Right now I’m deepening my footing in deep learning and shipping projects end-to-end — from a notebook to a deployed app people can actually click.
